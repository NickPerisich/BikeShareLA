About

My name is Nicholas Perisich and I am currently a sophomore at UNC Chapel Hill. This web app looks at metrics pertaining to the bike data from LA given to us in this challenge. The top section looks at the entire data set, looking at station, customer, and ride statistics. The next section filters the data down to summer and winter sets and looks to see if there is any difference in relevant statistics, particularly pertaining to the rides themselves and the customers who use them. This web app was generated using Spring Boot and a heavily modified HTML/CSS/JS front end that I found on the internet. I used Thymeleaf to serve the statistics from Spring Boot to the front end in case we wanted to use a CSV file (in the same format) with different data inside of it.

From the results, it is pretty easy to ascertain that station 3053 is heavily unpopular and should be moved if at all possible. Most riders, around 70%, are regular riders, so the bike share company should be able to rely on a reasonable amount of revenue each month. The usage time tends to be around 30 minutes in length, showing that this is a popular method of getting to and from somewhat un-walkable distances. Since the majority of riders are monthly pass holders who typically take round trips for somewhat long periods of time, it is safe to say that most riders use these bikes for everyday errands.
